DHJS 6-Month Study Plan

April 7 – October 2026 | Week-by-week schedule based on analysis of 11 years of actual question papers

📌 Study Strategy Principles

⏰ Daily Target: 6–8 hours
📖 Method: Bare Acts + MCQ Practice
🔄 Revision: Every Sunday = Revision Day
📝 Mains: Write 2 answers daily from Week 5
⚖️ Priority: Bare Acts > Commentary > Notes
🎯 Focus: Tier 1 topics = 60% of study time

📅 Month 1: April 7 – May 6, 2026 — Criminal Law Foundation I

IPC + CrPC Core
Week 1
Apr 7–13

IPC: General Principles

  • Read IPC Sections 1–120: General Exceptions, Common Intention (S.34), Abetment (S.107–109)
  • Conspiracy (S.120A–120B): definition, elements, punishment
  • Landmark cases: State of Maharashtra v. M.H. George, Barendra Kumar Ghosh v. King Emperor
  • Practice 20 MCQs on general principles daily
  • Bare Act reading: Mark all provisos and exceptions in IPC
Week 2
Apr 14–20

IPC: Offences Against Person — Most Tested Area

  • Murder vs. Culpable Homicide (S.299–302): all 5 exceptions to S.300
  • Dowry Death (S.304-B): elements, presumption under S.113-B Evidence Act
  • Rape & Sexual Offences (S.375–376E): post-2013 amendments
  • Grievous Hurt, Kidnapping, Abduction (S.320–369)
  • Practice scenario-based questions (Mains pattern)
Week 3
Apr 21–27

IPC: Property Offences + BNS 2023 Comparison

  • Theft, Robbery, Dacoity, Extortion (S.378–402): distinguish each carefully
  • Cheating, Criminal Breach of Trust, Mischief (S.415–443)
  • BNS 2023: key changes — Section numbers, new offences (S.111 organized crime, S.113 terrorism)
  • Create a comparison table: IPC vs BNS section numbers
  • Practice 30 IPC MCQs (mixed topics)
Week 4
Apr 28–May 6

CrPC Core — Jurisdiction, Investigation, Bail MILESTONE TEST

  • CrPC S.154–176: FIR, Investigation, Inquest (role, admissibility of FIR)
  • Bail: S.436–437 (bailable/non-bailable), S.167 default bail, S.439 High Court bail
  • Anticipatory Bail S.438: conditions, Gurbaksh Singh Sibbia principles
  • Cognizance: S.190–204, Police report vs. Complaint cases
  • MILESTONE TEST 1: 50 MCQs (IPC + CrPC basics) — Target 70%+

📅 Month 2: May 7 – June 6, 2026 — Criminal Law II + Evidence

CrPC Trial + Evidence Act
Week 5
May 7–13

CrPC: Trial Procedure & Special Laws

  • Sessions Trial (S.225–237), Summons Trial, Summary Trial
  • Charge: S.211–224, framing of charge, discharge, trial
  • S.313 examination, S.311 power to summon witnesses
  • PC Act 1988: S.7, S.13, sanction S.19, Trap cases
  • POCSO Act 2012: key offences, child-friendly procedures
Week 6
May 14–20

Evidence Act — Most Critical for Mains

  • Relevancy: S.6–55 (res gestae S.6, admissions S.17–23, confessions S.24–30)
  • Dying Declaration S.32(1): Laxman v. State of Maharashtra principles
  • Expert Opinion S.45: Pulukuri Kottaya on S.27 discovery
  • Electronic Evidence: S.65B certificate — Arjun Panditrao Khotkar case
  • Write 2 answer problems on Evidence daily
Week 7
May 21–27

Evidence Act: Proof, Burden, Presumptions

  • Oral/Documentary evidence: S.59–73A
  • Burden of Proof: S.101–114A (all statutory presumptions)
  • S.113A (abetment to suicide), S.113B (dowry death presumption)
  • Examination of witnesses: S.135–167, hostile witness, leading questions
  • Practice: 30 Evidence MCQs + write Mains problem answer
Week 8
May 28–June 6

BNSS 2023 + Consolidation MILESTONE TEST

  • BNSS 2023: key changes from CrPC — Zero FIR (15-day transfer), Forensics, Audio-video trial
  • BSA 2023 (replacing Evidence Act): key changes
  • Revision of all criminal laws
  • MILESTONE TEST 2: 75 MCQs (Full Criminal Law) — Target 70%+
  • Write one full Mains answer on criminal law problem

📅 Month 3: June 7 – July 6, 2026 — Civil Law Core

CPC + TPA + Constitutional Law
Week 9
June 7–13

CPC: Jurisdiction, Parties & Plaints

  • CPC: Jurisdiction (S.9–20), Res Judicata (S.11), Res Sub Judice (S.10)
  • Order VII: Plaint, rejection of plaint (Rule 11)
  • Order VIII: Written Statement, set-off, counter-claim
  • Order XXXIX: Temporary injunctions — principles (prima facie, balance, irreparable harm)
  • Practice 20 CPC MCQs daily
Week 10
June 14–20

CPC: Execution, Appeals, Revision

  • CPC: Order XXI (Execution of Decrees), S.96–100 (appeals), S.115 (revision)
  • Commercial Courts Act 2015: threshold, pre-institution mediation
  • Arbitration Act 1996: S.8, S.11, S.17, S.34, S.36 — key provisions
  • Motor Vehicles Act: MACT jurisdiction, S.166, compensation principles (Pranay Sethi)
Week 11
June 21–27

Transfer of Property Act + Specific Relief

  • TPA: S.5 (immovable property), S.52 (lis pendens), S.53A (part performance)
  • TPA: Mortgage types (S.58), Lease (S.105), Gift (S.122), Exchange
  • Specific Relief Act 2018: S.10 (specific performance as general rule), S.14 (bars)
  • Injunctions: S.36–42 SRA — perpetual, temporary, mandatory
Week 12
June 28–July 6

Constitutional Law MILESTONE TEST

  • Fundamental Rights: Art.14–32, reasonable classification test
  • Writs: Habeas Corpus, Mandamus, Certiorari, Prohibition, Quo Warranto
  • Art.21 expanded: right to life, privacy (Puttaswamy), livelihood
  • Art.32 vs Art.226, DPSPs, Basic Structure doctrine
  • MILESTONE TEST 3: 75 MCQs (Full Civil Law) — Target 70%+

📅 Month 4: July 7 – August 6, 2026 — Special Laws Sprint

HMA + Contract + NI Act + Labour + IP
Week 13
July 7–13

Family & Personal Laws

  • Hindu Marriage Act: S.5 (conditions), S.11 (void), S.12 (voidable), grounds for divorce S.13
  • Hindu Succession Act 1956: coparcenary, S.6 (amendment 2005), daughters' rights
  • Indian Succession Act: wills, probate, intestate succession
  • Surrogacy Act 2021 + PCPNDT Act key provisions
  • Write one Mains family law problem answer
Week 14
July 14–20

Contract Act + NI Act

  • ICA 1872: S.2 (definitions), S.10 (valid contract), S.27 (restraint of trade)
  • S.56 (frustration), S.73–74 (damages), S.124–147 (indemnity, guarantee)
  • NI Act S.138: ingredients, notice, time limits, S.139 presumption
  • Case: Meters and Instruments v. Kanchan Mehta (2018) — NI Act trial
  • Practice 25 NI Act + Contract MCQs
Week 15
July 21–27

Labour Laws + SARFAESI

  • Industrial Disputes Act 1947: workman definition, industrial dispute, conciliation
  • Four Labour Codes 2019–2020: scope and key changes
  • SARFAESI Act 2002: S.13 (60-day notice), S.17 (appeal to DRT)
  • DRT Act 1993 + RDB Act 1993: jurisdiction
  • Practice 20 Labour + SARFAESI MCQs
Week 16
July 28–Aug 6

IP Laws + IT Act MILESTONE TEST

  • Patents Act 1970: S.3 (non-patentable), S.25 (opposition), term = 20 years
  • Copyright Act 1957: term (life+60 years), infringement, fair dealing
  • Trademarks Act 1999: well-known marks, passing off
  • IT Act 2000: S.66 (hacking), S.67 (obscenity), S.43A (data protection)
  • MILESTONE TEST 4: 100 MCQs (All subjects mixed) — Target 75%+

📅 Month 5: August 7 – September 6, 2026 — Mock Tests & Mains Writing

Full-Length Tests + Answer Writing
Week 17–18

Full Prelim Mock Tests

  • Take one 150-Q mock test every 3 days (3-hour timed)
  • Analyze wrong answers: subject-wise error log
  • Target: maintain 75%+ accuracy consistently
  • Revise weak subjects based on mock test analysis
Week 19–20

Mains Answer Writing Practice

  • Daily: Write 4 Mains-style answers (2 civil + 2 criminal problems)
  • Focus: legal analysis, issue spotting, IRAC structure
  • GK Paper: Essay writing practice (Constitution, law reforms, current affairs)
  • Time yourself: 45 minutes per question target
  • Previous year mains papers: 2022, 2023 full papers

📅 Month 6: September 7 – October 2026 — Final Revision & Exam Sprint

Revision + Previous Papers
Week 21–22

Rapid Revision — Bare Acts

  • One-day rapid revision per subject (12 subjects in 12 days)
  • Focus: section numbers, key definitions, landmark cases
  • Flashcards: 10 key sections per subject
  • BNS/BNSS/BSA 2023: complete revision of new criminal laws
Week 23–24

Previous Year Papers — Full Analysis FINAL PREP

  • Solve DHJS 2024, 2023, 2022 Prelim papers under exam conditions
  • Analyze every error — no concept left behind
  • GK: Current legal developments (recent SC judgments, new legislation)
  • Maintain 80%+ accuracy in last 3 mock tests
  • Day before exam: Light revision only, good sleep, stay calm
